About

Researching tractable structure inside hard graph problems

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at IIT Indore. My research interests include parameterized algorithms, fixed-parameter tractable algorithm design, kernelization, and graph theory.

I am currently working on the Disconnected Matching problem. My work studies its complexity on special graph classes and investigates efficient algorithms under meaningful structural parameterizations.

Research

Research themes

My work combines structural graph insights with parameterized complexity techniques.

01

Parameterized Algorithms

Designing algorithms with running time f(k) · nO(1), where the parameter captures a useful feature of the input.

02

Kernelization

Developing safe reduction rules that convert an instance into a smaller equivalent instance bounded by a function of the parameter.

03

Graph Algorithms

Studying matching, domination, connectivity, and simultaneous-property problems on structured graph classes.
